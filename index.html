Projects


Optimal Network Sizes for Most Robust Turing Patterns
Scientific Reports 2025
PAPER CODE

Many cellular patterns exhibit a reaction-diffusion component, suggesting that Turing instability may contribute to pattern formation. However, biological gene-regulatory pathways are more complex than simple Turing activator-inhibitor models and generally do not require fine-tuning of parameters as dictated by the Turing conditions. In this paper, we employ random matrix theory to analyze the Jacobian matrices of larger networks with robust statistical properties. Our analysis reveals that Turing patterns are more likely to occur by chance than previously thought and that the most robust Turing networks have an optimal size, consisting of only a handful of molecular species, thus significantly increasing their identifiability in biological systems.

H. Shaberi, A. Kappassov, A. Matas-Gil, R. G. Endres



A Mathematical Framework for Describing and Predicting Epistasis in a Synthetic Gene Circuit
CODE

Epistasis is the phenomenon in which the effect of genotype on phenotype is dependent on a combination of two or more mutations. It plays a significant role in contributing to the phenotypic differences between and within species. Studying epistasis can have various therapeutic applications. In this project, we try to predict epistasis and its effect a priori using mathematical modelling. We present epistasis predicted by our models and discuss their performance. Finally, we talk about alternative inference techniques that can be applied in future studies.

Supervisor: Dr. Ruben Perez-Carrasco
Contributors: Aibek Kappassov, Aaryan Bhatia, Robin Curnow, Chi Chun Chan
